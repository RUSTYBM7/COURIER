International Shipping

16 questions
We ship to over 220 countries and territories worldwide. Our global network includes partnerships with local logistics providers in each region to ensure reliable delivery and customs handling. Some remote areas may have extended delivery times. Check our shipping calculator for specific routes and estimated delivery times. For destinations not covered, contact our team for a custom solution.
We handle customs documentation as part of our international service. During label creation, you provide accurate item descriptions, values, quantities, and Harmonized System (HS) codes for each item. Our system generates compliant customs forms automatically (commercial invoice, packing list, and customs declaration). For complex shipments, our customs experts can advise on duties, restrictions, and required documentation. Delays at customs are beyond our control but we'll help you resolve them.
By default, the recipient pays customs duties and taxes upon delivery. However, you can choose to DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) at checkout — meaning you pay these fees in advance. DDP is recommended for business shipments to ensure smooth delivery without recipient delays. If the recipient refuses to pay, the shipment may be held at customs or returned — all fees apply in either case.
Standard international shipments require: commercial invoice (generated by our system), packing list, and customs declaration. Additional documents may be needed for specific goods: certificates of origin, import licences, health certifications, or phytosanitary certificates for food/plants. Our system will prompt you for any extra documents based on the destination country and goods type. Missing documents are a common cause of customs delays.
Our shipping calculator provides estimates based on the weight, dimensions, origin, and destination you enter. The final cost may differ due to: dimensional weight adjustments at pickup, fuel surcharges applied at time of booking, destination surcharges (remote areas), and customs duties/taxes (which we cannot guarantee in advance). The quote at checkout is binding — the price you see is the price you'll pay, excluding recipient-paid duties.
If a package is held at customs, you'll be notified via email and the tracking status will update. Common reasons include: missing or incorrect documentation, restricted goods, undervaluation, or random inspection. Contact our customs team immediately — we can liaise with the destination customs authority on your behalf. Resolution time varies from 2 days to 4 weeks depending on the country and reason. Storage fees may apply after a holding period.
Yes — GETOnline and ShipNow accounts support bulk shipments to multiple international addresses. Use our CSV import feature to upload a list of recipients, or use our API to integrate with your order management system. Each address will generate a separate label with the correct customs documentation. Bulk discounts apply for 50+ shipments per month — contact our team for a custom quote.
HS (Harmonized System) codes are 6-10 digit numbers that classify goods for customs. You can search for codes at hscode.org or the WCO database. Using the wrong HS code can cause customs delays, fines, or seizure. Our system provides suggested HS codes based on your item description — we recommend verifying these against the destination country's tariff schedule. For high-value or complex shipments, consult our customs team for a classification review.
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — The sender pays all duties, taxes, and customs clearance fees in advance. The recipient receives the package with no additional payment required. Recommended for B2C shipments where the recipient is a consumer.

DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid) — The recipient pays applicable duties and taxes upon delivery. The sender only pays shipping costs. Recommended for B2B shipments where the recipient is a business with an established import process.
International packages are returned to sender when: the recipient refuses delivery, the address is incorrect or incomplete, customs cannot clear the shipment and the sender doesn't respond to notices within the holding period, or the goods are prohibited at the destination. Return shipping fees are non-refundable and will be charged to the sender's account. Contact support if your package has been returned unexpectedly.
Yes — our tracking system provides end-to-end visibility from pickup to delivery across all countries in our network. The tracking number works throughout the entire journey, including final-mile delivery by our local partner agents. You can also use our API to pull tracking events into your own system or embed tracking on your website for your customers.
Restrictions vary by country. Common restrictions include: food items (many countries require health certifications), electronics (some countries restrict certain frequencies or technologies), pharmaceuticals (strict licensing required), plant and animal products (phytosanitary certificates needed), and cultural artifacts (may require export permits from origin country). Always check the destination country's import regulations before shipping. Our system will warn you if a declared category has known restrictions.
Declare the actual transaction value — what you paid or are charging the recipient for the goods. Do not under-declare to reduce duties — this is illegal and can result in fines, seizure, or prosecution. For gifts or samples, declare the fair market retail value. Our system will help you complete the commercial invoice with item-level values. If there's no sale involved (e.g., returning goods), declare the original purchase price and note the reason.
No minimum value exists for customs purposes. However, some countries apply de minimis thresholds — below this value, no duties or taxes are charged. For example, United Kingdom's de minimis is GBP 400. Above this, import duties apply. Our system automatically calculates whether the destination's de minimis applies and shows you the estimated duties at checkout. Note: commercial shipments generally don't qualify for de minimis regardless of value.
Yes — if your business is registered for VAT in the destination country or in a country with a reciprocal VAT agreement, you can use your VAT number to simplify customs. In the EU, a VAT number allows you to import under the IOR (Importer of Record) scheme, which can reduce duties and simplify reporting. Enter your VAT number in the customs section at checkout. Our system validates VAT numbers automatically.
If the recipient refuses to pay customs duties, the package will be held at the destination customs depot. You (as the sender) will be given the option to: (1) authorise the package to be returned to you (return fees apply); (2) authorise the package to be abandoned or destroyed (no refund of shipping fees); (3) pay the duties to have the package released and reschedule delivery. You can set default instructions in your account settings for how to handle refused deliveries.

A package is considered lost in transit when there are no tracking updates for 7 days after the expected delivery date and our investigations with the destination carrier confirm the package cannot be located. Before declaring a loss, we first attempt to locate the package through our global carrier network. If after 30 days the package is still not found, a full claim can be processed. We will refund the shipping cost plus the declared value of the goods up to your coverage limit.

Standard coverage is £50 per shipment included free. Additional coverage can be purchased up to £5,000 per shipment for high-value items.
